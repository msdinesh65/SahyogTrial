A Journey from the Himalayas to the Big Apple

The Himalayan Roots

I had the privilege of being born and spending my formative years deep within the foothills of the Himalayas in India. Our idyllic small town was a haven, surrounded by the unrivaled beauty of the majestic mountains and the vibrant, life-giving flow of crystal-clear, snow-fed rivers. Life felt wonderfully uncomplicated and profoundly stress-free in that environment. The heart of my existence revolved around time spent with my close-knit family, and I treasure the memories created on the dusty fields where my friends and I enjoyed endless, spirited games of cricket.

Exploring the Holy Land

I was fortunate to arrive in Jerusalem in 1997 to begin my work as a postdoctoral fellow. The country was experiencing a unique juxtaposition of hope and tension; peace prospects shone brightly across the region, even as violence continued to mount. My personal life flourished during this time, as I met my wife at the Hebrew University. Driven by a shared curiosity, we traveled extensively through the surrounding lands—including Israel, Palestine, Jordan, and Egypt—gaining an invaluable, firsthand understanding of the deep historical currents and profound complexities that define the modern Middle East.

Big Apple Ambitions

My arrival in the Big Apple soon after 9/11 was a truly momentous experience. I was immediately drawn to the city's electric pace and the vast opportunities it presented, which served to accelerate my own ambitions. Following initial successes working with the United Nations Foundation and Columbia University, I felt compelled to take a significant leap. This resulted in the creation of my own nonprofit organization, which I grew into a $7 million enterprise in just three short years. However, such quick success brought its inevitable pitfalls, and I faced my own challenges. Nonetheless, my drive remains strong, and I continue to strive for impact in new ways, including through my participation in the Pursuit AI Native program.
